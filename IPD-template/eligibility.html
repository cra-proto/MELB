Eligibility

This section answers: “Am I allowed to do this?” It is one of the most important sections on any service page because it prevents users from wasting time on a process they cannot complete.

On this page

  • What to include
  • Guidance
  • Recommended headings

What to include

  • Who can use the service, apply for the benefit, or claim the credit
  • Who cannot (if exclusions are clearer than inclusions)
  • Specific criteria: income thresholds, age requirements, residency rules, filing status
  • Links to a more detailed eligibility tool or questionnaire if one exists

Guidance

  • Use clear, direct statements: “You can apply if you...” followed by a bulleted list of criteria
  • When eligibility is complex, consider using the interactive questions (fieldflow) pattern to walk users through a decision tree
  • Use “must” for legal requirements and “need to” for administrative requirements
  • Avoid “may be eligible” when you can be more specific
  • If eligibility depends on the tax year or a specific period, state the period explicitly
  • Place this section before the instructions — users should confirm eligibility before they invest time in a process

Recommended headings

  • Who can apply
  • Who can claim
  • Who is eligible
  • Who must register
  • Find out if you are eligible
