Introductory text

The first paragraph after the title. It tells the user whether they are in the right place.

On this page

  • What to include
  • Guidance

What to include

  • A one- or two-sentence plain-language description of what this page covers
  • What the service, benefit, or process is
  • Who it is broadly for (if not obvious from the title)

Guidance

  • Start with the most important information (inverted pyramid)
  • Write in second person: “You can use this form to...” not “This form allows taxpayers to...”
  • Do not repeat the title in the introductory text
  • Do not use this space for background, history, or policy context that the user does not need to complete their task
  • Keep it to 2–3 sentences maximum

Examples

Good introductory text

Write: “Find out if you are eligible for this one-time payment and how to apply. Most eligible people will receive the payment automatically.”

Introductory text to avoid

Don’t write: “The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) administers this benefit program on behalf of the Government of Canada, which was established under the Budget Implementation Act to provide financial assistance to eligible Canadians...”
