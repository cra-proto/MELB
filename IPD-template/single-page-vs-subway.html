Choosing a page format

Before you start building your page, decide how to structure your content. The choice between a single page and the subway (multi-page) pattern depends on the complexity and scope of the user's task.

Choose the format that best fits your content:

Single page format

Use when the content is manageable on one page, the process is straightforward, and users need most of the information in a single session.

Best for: simple claims, status checks, information updates, and processes with fewer than 8 steps.

Subway (multi-page) format

Use when the process has clearly distinct phases that users may complete at different times, and each phase has enough content to warrant its own page.

Best for: major benefits, tax credits, and services with complex eligibility, detailed instructions, and post-submission follow-up.

Use a single page

Your content is best suited to a single page with “On this page” navigation. Read the single page format guidance.

Use the subway pattern

Your content has enough distinct phases and complexity to benefit from the subway (multi-page) pattern. Read the subway format guidance.

A single page may still work

Even though your content has multiple phases, it may not need the subway pattern if users can manage it on one page. Consider testing with users before deciding. Review both the single page and subway format guidance.

Simplify or group your phases

More than 8 phases overwhelms users. Try grouping related phases together to get to 3–8 steps, then reassess. Review the subway format guidance for structuring tips.
