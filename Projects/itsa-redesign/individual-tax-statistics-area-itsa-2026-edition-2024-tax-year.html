Individual Tax Statistics by Area (ITSA) - 2026 Edition (2024 tax year)

This edition shows personal income tax data for the 2024 tax year, by city, region, and postal area in Canada. Data covers tax returns assessed up to November 30, 2025.

Tables are available in two formats: PDF (formatted for reading) and CSV (raw data for analysis).

Tasks these tables support

Use this section to choose the right table before downloading.

  • Count tax filers and the total income they reported in a given area for 2024
    Use Table 1: All returns filed
  • Compare income between male and female tax filers in a given city or region for 2024
    Use Table 2: All returns, males and Table 3: All returns, females
  • Break income down by source (employment, pension, investment, self-employment) for a given area in 2024
    Use Table 4: All returns, by source of income
  • Compare a specific city or region across editions (year-over-year)
    Download the same table from this edition and the 2025 edition (2023 tax year). Read the comparing-editions note before doing time-series analysis
  • Get the raw, reusable dataset to analyze in Excel, R, or Python
    Use the CSV files. Each CSV has one header row, then one row per geography and income class

Geographic areas explained

The 2026 edition uses geographic categories from the Statistics Canada 2021 Census. ITSA reports data for:

Census Metropolitan Area (CMA): a large city and its surrounding area
At least 100,000 residents in total, with 50,000 in the urban core. Surrounding municipalities are included if they have strong commuting links to the core. Each CMA has a unique identifier from 001 to 995.
Census Agglomeration (CA): a smaller city and its surrounding area
At least 10,000 residents in the urban core. Same commuting-link rule as CMAs.
Forward Sortation Area (FSA): first 3 characters of a postal code
For example, K1A for parts of Ottawa or H2X for parts of Montréal.
Census division and census subdivision
A census division is roughly a county or regional district. A census subdivision is roughly a municipality.
Province or territory
The 13 provinces and territories, plus an "all Canada" total.

Income classification

Income classes in the tables are based on total income assessed, which is line 15000 of the T1 return. It includes:

  • Employment income
  • Pension income
  • Investment income
  • Self-employment income
  • Social benefit payments
  • Other income

What total income does not include

Total income assessed does not include these non-taxable sources:

  • GST/HST credit and Canada Child Benefit payments, or related provincial and territorial program payments
  • Child assistance payments and the supplement for children with disabilities paid by Quebec
  • Compensation from a province or territory for victims of crime or motor vehicle accidents
  • Lottery winnings
  • Most gifts and inheritances
  • Disability or death payments from Canada or an ally for war service (if not taxable in that country)
  • Most amounts from life insurance following a death
  • Most union strike pay
  • Most withdrawals from a Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)

Income earned on any of these amounts is taxable.

How income amounts are measured

Total income assessed can differ from "true economic income" used in other publications, because it:

  • Excludes the non-taxable sources listed above
  • Includes grossed-up dividend income (eligible dividends are reported at value plus 38%)
  • Reports interest and investment income gross (carrying charges are not deducted)
  • Reports taxable capital gains as net amounts (only 50% of realized gains are reported)

Income source categories (used in Table 4)

Table 4 breaks income down by source. Each source category combines several lines of the T1 return.

Employment income

  • Line 10100: employment income from T4 slips (box 14), including commissions on line 10120
  • Line 10400: other employment income

Pension income

  • Line 11300: Old Age Security (OAS) pension. Monthly benefit for people 65 and over (box 18, T4A(OAS) slip)
  • Line 11400: Canada Pension Plan (CPP) or Quebec Pension Plan (QPP) benefits, including death, disability, survivor, and children's benefits (box 20, T4A(P) slip)
  • Line 11500: other pensions and superannuation, including registered pension plans, registered retirement income funds, deferred profit-sharing plans, and foreign pensions
  • Line 11600: elected split-pension amount (from Form T1032). Methodology note: pension amounts in this edition were calculated by reducing line 11600 by the deduction for elected split-income (line 21000) that is already included. Do not compare pension income across this edition and editions before 2021 without accounting for this change

Investment income

  • Line 12000: taxable dividends from Canadian corporations (with gross-up: 38% for eligible dividends, 18% for non-eligible)
  • Line 12100: interest and other investment income
  • Line 12200: net partnership income (limited or non-active partners only)
  • Line 12600: net rental income (after expenses)
  • Line 12700: taxable capital gains (50% of realized gains). Excludes capital losses for deceased filers, which are deducted later at line 26000

Self-employment income

Self-employment income is net (gross income minus adjustments and expenses).

  • Line 13500: net business income (businesses and partnerships)
  • Line 13700: net professional income (independent practice: accountants, doctors, dentists, lawyers)
  • Line 13900: net commission income (for example, real estate agents)
  • Line 14100: net farming income (including beekeeping and tree farming)
  • Line 14300: net fishing income (boat owners, crew, or shore fishers)

Social benefit payments

  • Line 11900: employment insurance and other benefits (box 14, T4E slip)
  • Line 14400: workers' compensation benefits (T5007 slip)
  • Line 14500: social assistance payments (T5007 slip)
  • Line 14600: net federal supplement: allowance, allowance for the survivor, or Guaranteed Income Supplement (box 21, T4A(OAS) slip)

Other income

  • Line 12500: Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) income (box 28, T4A slip)
  • Line 12800: taxable part of support payments received
  • Line 12900: RRSP income
  • Lines 12905 and 12906: First Home Savings Account (FHSA) income
  • Line 13000: other income (apprenticeship grants, lump-sum payments from pensions, retiring allowances, death benefits other than CPP or QPP, and more, see the 2024 General Income Tax and Benefit Guide)
  • Line 13010: taxable scholarships, fellowships, bursaries, and artists' project grants

Gender

Gender is recorded as female, male, gender diverse, or unknown. Non-binary individuals are counted in the gender diverse category. Tax filers for whom no gender code is available (for example, non-residents with an Individual Tax Number) are counted as unknown. To protect confidentiality, this publication reports only the female and male categories.

Data source and confidentiality

Where the data comes from

Data are taken from income tax returns and related schedules filed for the 2024 tax year:

  • T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return
  • T1S-D, Credit and Benefit Return (for Indians registered, or eligible to be registered, under the Indian Act)

How taxpayer confidentiality is protected

  • Counts are rounded to the nearest 10
  • Dollar amounts are rounded to the nearest $1,000
  • Values are suppressed where releasing them could identify individual taxpayers
  • A zero (0) in any cell indicates a suppressed value (including true zeros)

Comparing this edition to previous ones

Read this before doing year-over-year analysis.

Starting with this edition, an earlier extraction cut-off date is used to publish the statistics sooner:

  • 2026 edition: data extracted on November 30, 2025
  • 2025 edition and earlier: data extracted on June 30 of the year of publication

This means the 2026 edition includes fewer late-filed and reassessed returns than older editions. Account for this when comparing across editions.

Pension income calculations also changed in the 2021 edition: line 11600 (elected split-pension amount) was reduced by the deduction for elected split-income (line 21000). Do not compare pension income in this edition with editions before 2021 without accounting for this change.

For a full list of previous editions, return to the ITSA landing page.
