Individual Tax Statistics by Area (ITSA)

The Individual Tax Statistics by Area (ITSA) dataset shows how many people file taxes, how much income they report, and which credits they claim, broken down by where they live in Canada.

Use ITSA to compare personal income tax data across cities, regions, postal areas, and provinces or territories. Each edition is published as PDF tables and as raw CSV data you can analyze in your own tools.

If you came here looking for your own tax information

ITSA does not show personal accounts or individual returns. If you want your own Notice of Assessment, refund status, or benefit payments, sign in to My Account (CRA).

What ITSA can and cannot answer

Each item below is a yes-or-no capability. Green checks show what ITSA covers. Red X marks show what it does not.

  • Count tax filers in a given city, region, or postal area
  • See total and average income reported in a given geography
  • Break income down by source (employment, pension, investment, self-employment)
  • Compare tax filers across cities for a given tax year
  • Track how an area's tax data has changed across editions
  • ITSA does not show Individual taxpayer records
  • ITSA does not show Business or corporate tax returns
  • ITSA does not show GST/HST registrant data
  • ITSA does not show Personal benefit payment data

For the topics with X marks, see the Income statistics and GST/HST statistics hub.

Geographic terms used in ITSA

ITSA reports tax data using geographic categories from the Statistics Canada 2021 Census. Definitions are below.

Forward Sortation Area (FSA)

The first 3 characters of a postal code (for example, K1A for parts of Ottawa). Use this for postal-code-level analysis.

Census Metropolitan Area (CMA)

A large city and its surrounding area. Must have at least 100,000 residents, with 50,000 in the urban core. Examples: Toronto CMA, Montréal CMA, Vancouver CMA.

Census Agglomeration (CA)

A smaller city and its surrounding area. Must have at least 10,000 residents in the urban core. Examples: Kingston CA, Moncton CA.

Census Metropolitan Influenced Zone (CMIZ)

An area outside a CMA or CA, classified by the share of residents who commute into a CMA or CA for work.

Census division and census subdivision

Larger groupings used by Statistics Canada. A census division is roughly a county or regional district. A census subdivision is roughly a municipality.

Province or territory

The 13 Canadian provinces and territories. ITSA tables also include an "all Canada" total.

About edition naming: Each ITSA edition is published 2 years after the tax year it covers. For example, the 2026 Edition reports data from the 2024 tax year. This is because the CRA waits for most returns and reassessments to be processed before publishing the dataset.

Comparing editions: Starting with the 2026 edition, an earlier extraction cut-off date is used (November 30 of the year following the tax year, versus June 30 of the year of publication in earlier editions). Account for this when comparing across editions.
