Information architecture changes – How a COP is developed

This is the core of what a senior designer does in a COP. Below are the exact before-and-after page structures for 6 COPs, showing how experienced designers restructured content to improve findability and task completion.

On this page

  • GST/HST Business – the gold standard
  • Collections & Payment (CAP) – from flat to deep
  • End of Life (EOL) – from 6 pages to 50+
  • TFSA – topic page optimization
  • T1 Personal Income Tax – broadest scope
  • Business Registration – most heavily iterated

GST/HST Business – the gold standard

This is the only COP with a formal CHANGELOG tracking every add/delete/modify with exact old and new Canada.ca URLs. 66 pages in scope, net result: 36 deleted, 3 added, 22 modified.

Before: Technology-organized silos (66 pages across 2 sections)

Each filing method (NETFILE, Telefile, IFT, EDI) had its own section with identical scaffolding repeated 4 times:

  • NETFILE silo – 10 pages: About, Before you start, Eligibility, FAQ (landing), FAQ: Eligibility, FAQ: General, FAQ: Using the service, Ready to file, Adjust a return, Get access code, Feedback
  • IFT silo – 8 pages: About, Before you start, Eligibility, FAQ (landing), FAQ: General, FAQ: Eligibility, FAQ: Using the service, Feedback
  • Telefile silo – 7 pages: About, Before you start, Eligibility, FAQ (landing), FAQ: Eligibility, FAQ: General, FAQ: Using the service
  • EDI silo – 3 pages: About, FAQ, Who can use

All of these lived under digital-services-businesses/. A separate section under gst-hst-businesses/ had the topic page and a subway structure for the filing process.

The core problem: A business owner looking for "how do I file?" had to first navigate to the right technology silo, then wade through 7+ pages of method-specific content. The same questions were answered 4 times.

After: Task-organized hierarchy (33 pages – net reduction of 33 pages)

The restructured content is organized by what the user needs to do:

  • Calculate and prepare to report (new hub)
    • Which return to use
    • Instructions for completing a return
    • Records to keep
    • Software for IFT
  • File your GST/HST return (subway navigation)
    • Reporting requirements and deadlines
      • Filing penalties
    • How to file (with one page per method)
      • In your CRA account (new)
      • Using NETFILE form (replaces 10-page silo)
      • Using third-party software (replaces 8-page silo)
      • By phone / TELEFILE (replaces 7-page silo)
      • Through financial institution (replaces 3-page silo)
      • By mail (new)
      • Get access code
    • After you file
    • Contact us (renamed from "What if you have a complaint")
  • Remit/pay (consolidates 4 separate payment pages into 1)

What the designers did

GST/HST transformation logic
Transformation Before After Why
Eliminated duplicate scaffolding 4 silos × (About + Before + Eligibility + FAQ×3 + Feedback) = 28 pages 0 pages (content absorbed) Same questions answered 4 times
Consolidated filing methods Each method was a mini-site Each method is one page User task is "file my return" not "learn about NETFILE"
Moved from digital-services to topic section Filing methods under e-services/digital-services/ Under gst-hst-businesses/file-return/how-file/ Users look under "GST/HST" not "digital services"
Consolidated payments 4 pages: how, when, correct, check balance 1 page All payment info on one page
Added pre-filing hub Prepare info scattered New calculate-prepare section Separates "prepare" from "file"
Renamed complaint page "What if you have a complaint" "Contact us" Task-based, less adversarial

Collections & Payment (CAP) – from flat to deep

Before: 8 flat pages
  • Payments to the CRA (index)
  • Send a payment
  • Find a payment
  • Online payment options
  • Mail payment options
  • In-person payment options
  • Payment arrangements
  • Payment collections
After: 120+ pages in 4 deep sections
  • Make a payment (15+ pages) – One page per specific payment method: online banking, debit at CRA, ATM, Canada Post, mail, bank teller, third-party, pre-authorized debit, wire transfer, remittance voucher, plus accessibility variants
  • Corporate instalments (12+ pages) – Built from nothing: when to pay, calculate amount, worksheets, federal/provincial, special situations, how to pay, due dates, interest & penalties, claim on return, change amount, follow up
  • Debt collections (7 pages) – Built from nothing: types of collection actions, garnishment, liens and seizures, co-liability, offsets, RTP/DTP, limitation period
  • Unable to pay (3+ pages) – Dedicated section: how to reduce debt, call about debt
  • Supporting pages – Confirm a payment, uncashed cheques, refunds, interest and penalties, payment arrangements, verify amount owing

Key IA transformations

  • 8 flat pages → 4 deep sections (make-payment, corporate-instalments, debt-collections, unable-pay)
  • Payment methods exploded: 3 generic "options" pages → 12+ specific method pages
  • Corporate instalments built from nothing: Entire 12-page section created
  • Landing page was the hardest problem: 8+ index variants tested
  • Release snapshots preserved: "release1" files mark what went to production first

End of Life (EOL) – from 6 pages to 50+

Before: 6-page skeleton
  • What to do following a death (index)
  • Represent someone who died
  • List of assets
  • Manage estate debt
  • Prepare returns for someone who died
  • Types and deadlines
After: 50+ page journey-based structure
  • Notify CRA of a death (new – a major gap in the original)
  • Represent someone who died (expanded)
  • List of assets (expanded)
  • Manage estate debt (expanded)
  • Prepare returns (now a gateway to sub-sections)
    • Types of returns and deadlines
    • Due date calculator (new)
    • Report income and assets – split into 14+ pages by income type: employment, pension, investment, employment insurance, death benefits, farm/fishing, property/capital gains, RRSP, RRIF, RDSP, TFSA, rental
    • Credits and deductions (new) – charitable donations, capital losses, CPP contributions
    • How to file (new)
    • After you file (new)
  • Clearance certificate (1 page → 10 pages)
    • How to apply, Submit, Processing time, Why you need one, Consequences of not getting one, plus entity-specific pages for corporations, GST/HST, trusts
  • CPP death benefit (new)
  • Business death/retirement (new)

Key IA transformations

  • Clearance certificate: 1 → 10 pages – Split by task and entity type
  • Report assets: 1 → 14+ pages – Split by income type
  • Credits/deductions: 0 → 4 pages – Entirely new section
  • Filing journey created – Linear progression: types/deadlines → due dates → report assets → credits → file → after you file
  • Notify-death page added – There was no page about how to actually tell CRA someone died

TFSA – topic page optimization

After: Deep topic structure (35+ pages including variants)
  • TFSA topic page (4 layout variants tested)
  • What is a TFSA
  • Opening a TFSA
  • Contributing to a TFSA (section landing)
    • Before you contribute (+ variant)
    • Calculate your contribution room (+ variant)
    • How to contribute
    • Permitted TFSA investments (added Oct 2025)
    • What to do if you contribute too much
  • Withdrawing from a TFSA
  • Requesting a transfer
  • If you become non-resident
  • Owing tax on a TFSA (section landing)
    • If you have to pay tax
    • If you have excess contributions (4 split variants explored)
    • Non-resident contributions
    • Non-permitted investments
    • Payment options (added Jul 2025)
  • When a TFSA holder dies (renamed from "if")
    • What happens when a holder dies
    • If you are a successor holder (+ variant)
    • If you are a beneficiary

Key IA transformations

  • "If" → "When": if-tfsa-holder-dies renamed to when-a-tfsa-holder-dies – more empathetic framing
  • Excess contributions splitting: Explored breaking one dense page into up to 5 separate pages
  • Payment options added: New page for how to actually pay TFSA taxes owed
  • Permitted investments added late: A gap identified 13 months into the project
  • Definitions system created: Custom definitions file for consistent terminology

T1 Personal Income Tax – broadest scope

Structure overview (~120 pages in a flat directory)
  • Get Ready – Get ready to do your taxes, Who should file, Important dates, Tax slips, Reporting income
  • How to File (the core journey)
    • How to file (hub)
    • Certified tax software – plus 16 individual software product pages (one per product)
    • EFILE, NETFILE overview, SimpleFile pilot, Paper return
    • Sub-step pages (subway navigation): Auto-fill, Complete, NETFILE, Send, Express NOA
  • After Filing – After you file, Notice of assessment, Refunds, Balance owing, Change your return, Review
  • CRA Services – Login services, Digital services, E-services, Help
  • Deductions/Credits – Claiming deductions, Credits and expenses, Child care expenses

Key observation: T1 is notable for its 16 individual software product pages – a level of detail no other COP has. This suggests the "which software should I use?" question was a major user pain point.

IA transformation strategies

Looking across all 6 COPs, senior designers consistently applied these strategies:

Cross-COP IA transformation patterns
Pattern Where applied Example
Consolidate duplicate scaffolding GST/HST (28 pages → 0) 4 filing method silos each with About/FAQ/Eligibility → 1 page per method
Split by income/entity type EOL (1 → 14 income pages, 1 → 9 clearance pages) Single page → one page per type
Add missing journey steps EOL (notify-death), GST/HST (mail filing, CRA account), TFSA (payment-options) Gaps identified through user research
Rename for empathy/clarity TFSA ("if" → "when" holder dies), GST/HST ("complaint" → "contact us") Language that matches user mental models
Create section landing pages All COPs Gateway pages that orient users before diving into sub-pages
Move from org-structured to task-structured GST/HST, CAP Users think in tasks, not org charts
Heavy landing page iteration CAP (8), Business Reg. (5), GST/HST (7+), TFSA (4) The entry point is always the hardest design problem
Numbered file variants, not branches All COPs _proto0 = baseline, _proto1+ = proposed, archived to /old/

The consistent thread: Every COP moves from technology/organization-structured content to task/user-journey-structured content, and the landing page always takes the most iterations to get right.
