Report payments on a T4A-NR slip

If you paid a non-resident for services provided in Canada and withheld Regulation 105 tax, you must report the payment on a T4A-NR slip.

File the T4A-NR information return by the last day of February following the calendar year in which you made the payment. Give the non-resident their copy of the T4A-NR slip by the same deadline.

For more details, refer to Guide RC4445, T4A-NR – Payments to Non-Residents for Services Provided in Canada.

When to use an NR4 slip instead

Use an NR4 slip instead of (or in addition to) a T4A-NR slip when:

  • you withheld Part XIII tax on payments such as acting services in a film or video production
  • the CRA authorized a reduced withholding rate and instructed you to report on an NR4

If you received CRA authorization to reduce withholding for a non-resident actor, report those amounts on an NR4 slip using exemption code "J".

For more details, refer to Guide T4061, NR4 – Non-Resident Tax Withholding, Remitting, and Reporting.

How to remit the withheld tax

Remit the withheld tax to the CRA by the 15th day of the month following the month you made the payment.

Use your non-resident tax account number to remit. You can remit online through your CRA My Business Account, through your financial institution, or by mail.
