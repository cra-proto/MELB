Payers who must withhold

If you pay fees, commissions, or other amounts to a non-resident for services provided in Canada, you must withhold tax.

The non-resident you pay can be an individual, corporation, partnership, or trust.

Common payers who must withhold include:

  • Canadian businesses that hire non-resident contractors or consultants
  • Event organizers that pay non-resident performers or speakers
  • Production companies that pay non-resident actors or crew
  • Canadian corporations that pay fees to non-resident directors
  • Non-resident businesses that hire sub-contractors to work in Canada

Payments that are covered

Under paragraph 153(1)(g) of the Income Tax Act and Regulation 105, you must withhold tax on fees, commissions, and other amounts paid to non-residents for services rendered in Canada. For the CRA's full guidance, see IC75-6, Required withholding from amounts paid to non-residents providing services in Canada. These include:

  • fees for professional services (such as consulting, legal, accounting, or engineering)
  • commissions
  • management or administration fees
  • technical service fees
  • payments for artistic or athletic performances
  • payments for acting services in film or video productions
  • director's fees paid to non-resident directors of Canadian corporations

Director's fees

Director's fees are generally subject to Regulation 102 withholding (payroll deductions at graduated rates) rather than the flat 15% Regulation 105 rate. You report these on a T4 slip, not a T4A-NR. If you are unsure which regulation applies, contact the CRA.

When services are considered to be in Canada

Services are considered to be provided in Canada when any part of the work is physically performed in Canada. This applies even if:

  • the contract was signed outside Canada
  • the non-resident's head office is outside Canada
  • the payment is made outside Canada
  • the final product or deliverable is used outside Canada

If a non-resident provides services partly in Canada and partly outside Canada, you only withhold on the portion of the payment for services provided in Canada. You must be able to support the allocation with documentation, such as time records or the terms of the contract.

Exceptions to withholding

You do not need to withhold Regulation 105 tax in the following situations:

  • the CRA has approved a waiver or reduction of withholding for the non-resident (see Apply for a waiver or reduction)
  • you are reimbursing the non-resident for travel or living expenses that the non-resident incurred while providing services in Canada, as long as the reimbursement is reasonable and supported by receipts
  • the payment is for goods only, with no service component

If a treaty would have allowed a lower rate of tax, the non-resident can file a Canadian income tax return to claim a refund of any tax withheld in excess of their actual tax liability.
